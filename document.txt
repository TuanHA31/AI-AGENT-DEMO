Southeast Asia – Overview of 11 Countries

Southeast Asia consists of 11 countries located between the Indian Ocean and the Pacific Ocean. The region is known for cultural diversity, economic growth, tropical climate, and strategic trade routes.

----------------------------------------
1. Vietnam
Capital: Hanoi
Population: Over 100 million
Government: Socialist republic
Currency: Vietnamese dong (VND)
Main language: Vietnamese
Economy: Manufacturing, electronics, textiles, agriculture, exports
Notable facts: One of the fastest-growing economies in ASEAN. Major exporter of rice and coffee.

----------------------------------------
2. Thailand
Capital: Bangkok
Population: Over 70 million
Government: Constitutional monarchy
Currency: Thai baht (THB)
Main language: Thai
Economy: Tourism, automotive industry, agriculture
Notable facts: Famous for tourism, temples, and food. Major global tourist destination.

----------------------------------------
3. Indonesia
Capital: Jakarta
Population: Over 270 million
Government: Presidential republic
Currency: Indonesian rupiah (IDR)
Main language: Indonesian (Bahasa Indonesia)
Economy: Natural resources, palm oil, coal, manufacturing
Notable facts: Largest country in Southeast Asia by population and land area. An archipelago with over 17,000 islands.

----------------------------------------
4. Malaysia
Capital: Kuala Lumpur
Population: Over 33 million
Government: Federal constitutional monarchy
Currency: Malaysian ringgit (MYR)
Main language: Malay
Economy: Electronics, petroleum, palm oil
Notable facts: Diverse multicultural society with Malay, Chinese, and Indian communities.

----------------------------------------
5. Singapore
Capital: Singapore
Population: About 6 million
Government: Parliamentary republic
Currency: Singapore dollar (SGD)
Main language: English, Malay, Mandarin, Tamil
Economy: Finance, global trade, technology, port services
Notable facts: Major global financial center and one of the richest countries per capita.

----------------------------------------
6. Philippines
Capital: Manila
Population: Over 110 million
Government: Presidential republic
Currency: Philippine peso (PHP)
Main language: Filipino and English
Economy: Services, remittances, business process outsourcing (BPO)
Notable facts: Archipelago with over 7,000 islands. Strong overseas worker population.

----------------------------------------
7. Myanmar
Capital: Naypyidaw
Population: Over 50 million
Government: Military-influenced political system
Currency: Kyat (MMK)
Main language: Burmese
Economy: Agriculture, natural gas, jade
Notable facts: Political instability in recent years has affected economic development.

----------------------------------------
8. Cambodia
Capital: Phnom Penh
Population: Over 16 million
Government: Constitutional monarchy
Currency: Cambodian riel (KHR)
Main language: Khmer
Economy: Garment manufacturing, tourism, agriculture
Notable facts: Home to Angkor Wat, one of the largest religious monuments in the world.

----------------------------------------
9. Laos
Capital: Vientiane
Population: Over 7 million
Government: Socialist republic
Currency: Lao kip (LAK)
Main language: Lao
Economy: Hydropower, mining, agriculture
Notable facts: Landlocked country; relies heavily on river transport and hydropower.

----------------------------------------
10. Brunei
Capital: Bandar Seri Begawan
Population: Under 1 million
Government: Absolute monarchy
Currency: Brunei dollar (BND)
Main language: Malay
Economy: Oil and gas
Notable facts: One of the wealthiest countries in the region due to petroleum resources.

----------------------------------------
11. Timor-Leste
Capital: Dili
Population: About 1.3 million
Government: Semi-presidential republic
Currency: United States dollar (USD)
Main language: Tetum and Portuguese
Economy: Oil and gas, agriculture
Notable facts: One of the youngest countries in Asia, gained independence in 2002.

----------------------------------------
Regional Organization

ASEAN (Association of Southeast Asian Nations):
Founded: 1967
Members: All 10 Southeast Asian countries except Timor-Leste (observer status).
Purpose: Promote economic growth, political stability, and regional cooperation.

General Characteristics of Southeast Asia:
- Tropical climate
- High cultural and linguistic diversity
- Strategic shipping routes (e.g., Strait of Malacca)
- Rapid economic development in recent decades
